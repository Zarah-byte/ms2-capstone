Direction 01

The Living Archive

A family tree that looks like a family attic

Concept

Textured, warm, analog. Photos look like they were scanned from envelopes. Stories appear as handwritten notes.

Interaction Model

You navigate by opening things — a drawer, a folder, an envelope. Stories are layered like objects in a chest.

Design Argument

Memory is material. A database erases the patina of time — this interface restores it.

"Design a family history interface that feels like inheriting a box of old objects. Every interaction is an act of careful handling."
Warm / analog aesthetic Paper, ink, texture Archival metaphor Slow, deliberate navigation

Direction 02

The Constellation

Family as a universe of connected moments

Concept

People are nodes of light in an open field. Stories are the threads between them. No hierarchy — no top, no root.

Interaction Model

You drift through the space. People cluster by era, geography, or theme. Clicking a story lights up every person it touches.

Design Argument

The hierarchical family tree is a colonial artifact. A constellation has no root. Every star is equally present in the dark.

"Design a family history interface with no hierarchy and no root. People exist in space, not in rows. The navigation is gravitational."
Spatial / non-hierarchical Dark canvas, light nodes Gravitational navigation Story-first topology

Direction 03

The Unreliable Narrator

An interface that makes disagreement visible

Concept

Every story has a teller. The interface foregrounds who is speaking and what they don't know. Conflicting memories stack.

Interaction Model

You read a story in layers. The first is the account. The second is the gaps. The third is the contradiction — another version.

Design Argument

Family history is a contested oral tradition. The honest archive doesn't pretend to be authoritative. It shows the seams.

"Design a family history interface built around contested memory. Every story is attributed to a specific teller. Gaps are made visible."
Layered, multi-perspective Conflict as feature Attribution-first Absence made visible
